## Material corrections to the July 3 all-verticals report

Research agents were instructed to verify rather than confirm, and three findings change the report:

1. **Workers comp: the "40% initial claim submission error rate" is unsupported.** Two independent agents found no public source; verified initial *denial* rates are ~7-13% (up to 22% in some surveys), driven by disputes, not form errors. The 40%-class error numbers belong to *medical billing* — the insurer/provider side, a different product. **Do not use the 40% stat with any attorney buyer.**
2. **Workers comp: the FROI is filed by the employer, not the claimant attorney** — the "First Report of Injury consistency" pitch cannot be sold to the claimant side. The *repositioned* wedge is real (below).
3. **SSDI: the SSA EDPNA directory is internal (RASR system), not downloadable.** Scrapeable proxies: NOSSCR's find-a-rep directory, NADR public member profiles, and the EDPNA exam-prep pipeline (StratComm/becomeanedpna.com — reaches new reps before tool loyalty forms). Also: the report's "$16.5M raised by Advocate" is unverified; only a $4M seed is public. And a bigger DTC player exists: **Turnout, $35M Series A**.

Also strengthened: bankruptcy's Ch13 dismissal rate is **~50.6%** per the 2024 BAPCPA report (the report's 48% was conservative), and CY2025's 574,314 filings figure verified exactly.

## Workers comp (repositioned, then viable)

- **The real wedge:** not initial claims — the claimant attorney's *litigation-lifecycle* filings. California EAMS demands exact string-matched Uniform Assigned Names (misses go to a clerk purgatory queue); **DWC announced sanctions/suspensions for repeated e-filer errors in May 2024 — a regulator punishing form errors is the demand signal**. Illinois returns outdated forms; NY rejects stale form versions; C&R body-part scoping errors permanently cost clients.
- **Beachheads (volume-verified):** CA (679,537 FROI/2024) → NY (165,320 claims, 9M+ documents) → FL (69,676 attorney-driven petitions through mandatory e-filing; promoted over IL's ~40K).
- **Incumbent gap:** MerusCase (the CA applicant-side standard) fills and transmits EAMS forms but validates nothing — same shaped gap as Docketwise in immigration.
- **Channels:** WILG (1,100+ members; **Annual Convention Napa Oct 17-21, 2026** — bookable now, named exhibitor contact), IWBA (NY; 80%-claimant membership rule = purest buyer room; **CLE Sept 18-19 Saratoga**), CAAA (~1,200 members; 2026 booths sold out/waitlisted — 2027 play), WorkCompCentral self-serve ads from $80/wk (cheapest message test), FWA (Florida — June 2026 convention passed).
- **Anchor prospects:** Pacific Workers' (Oakland, 13K+ clients claimed — size borderline), Cardamone Law (PA-exclusive, 8-person), Anderson & Johnson (Sacramento — partner's direct email, bilingual), Kramer & Pollack (NYC, dedicated Spanish section). ICP flags: Shulman & Hill and Pacific Attorney Group verified over-ICP.
- **Sweep:** 28 claimant-side firms (15 CA / 8 NY / 5 IL); enrichment recovered all firm names Justia omitted and flagged 9 bilingual firms.

## Enrichment quality notes (what the no-guessing rule caught)

- Rejected throughout: ZoomInfo/search-snippet emails not verbatim on a firm page (7+ instances), template placeholders (`user@domain.com`), **font-license artifact emails embedded in website theme CSS**, a web developer's address on a firm contact page.
- Caught before outreach: one defunct SSDI firm (attorney moved to big-law), one "immigration" lead that's actually a DUI firm, one WC domain 301-redirecting to a multi-state PI firm, Butler Viadro mid-rebrand, stale/wrong URLs and phones in directory data (corrected with source).
- One flagged-but-kept oddity: a published email on a bankruptcy firm site with a likely typo'd domain (`@mcom.com`) — recorded as-seen with a warning.
