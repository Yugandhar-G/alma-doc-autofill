## Executive summary

The market splits into four lanes with very different competitive pressure:

1. **The "buyable middle" of law firms is the core direct-sales target.** Every immigration firm above ~$50M revenue (Fragomen, BAL, WR, Erickson, Ogletree) built a proprietary platform and won't buy. Everything below runs on Docketwise/INSZoom plus paralegal typing. The gap between those two — high-volume boutiques with 3:1+ paralegal-to-attorney ratios — is where the pain and the budget coexist.
2. **The nonprofit lane is high-volume and essentially uncontested.** No incumbent does vision extraction from documents; they all autofill from typed questionnaires. Grant money (USCIS CIGP up to $300K/award, state ONA programs) can pay for it.
3. **The corporate lane is the biggest but already crowded** with funded AI-native entrants — including a company literally named **Yunaki (tryyunaki.com, $5.1M raised)**. Name collision needs a branding decision before any corporate outreach.
4. **Partnership/embed is the fastest leverage.** Paradigm (LollyLaw + PracticePanther) is the only major immigration-CMS parent without shipped AI extraction; Mitratech just opened a named third-party AI ecosystem; paralegal-outsourcing ALSPs are a white-label channel with no procurement committee.

**Single best pitch stat:** AILA's 2025 Practice Pulse — 68% of immigration lawyers use GenAI personally, but firm-level adoption is ~17%. Firms want AI they can deploy defensibly; "mandatory human review, never auto-submits, null over guess" is exactly that. ([AILA via Docketwise](https://www.docketwise.com/blog/how-ai-is-impacting-immigration-lawyers-and-cases/))

**Strongest single fit signal found in public data:** paralegal-to-attorney ratio. Murthy 70 staff / 13 attorneys, Garfinkel 45 / 10, NPZ 17 / 6. High ratio = form-production factory = buyer.

### Funding mechanics

- USCIS Citizenship & Integration Grant Program: up to 40 awards of up to $300K each (FY2024); tech line items allowed → price annual contracts to grant envelopes. ([uscis.gov](https://www.uscis.gov/archive/fiscal-year-2024-citizenship-and-integration-grant-program))

- Anchors on willingness-to-pay: Cerenade offers a **50% nonprofit discount**; CitizenshipWorks is **free** for N-400 — so lead with multi-form document-to-form pipelines (G-28 + I-589 + I-485 packets), not N-400-only.
